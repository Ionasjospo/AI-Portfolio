Dataset: "Golf" and "Golf-TestSet"
In this task, I worked with two datasets, "Golf" and "Golf-TestSet". Both datasets contained numerical variables like "Humidity" and "Temperature". Here's how I processed them and tested the model using Naive Bayes:

Discretization:
I used the "Numerical to Polynomial" operator to transform numerical variables into categorical ones. This helps improve the model's accuracy.

After generating, training, and testing the Naive Bayes model, the following results were obtained:

  • ApplyModel: Showed the model's predictions along with confidence levels for each prediction, as well as the meteorological conditions for each example. The confidence values indicate how "sure" the model was in its predictions, helping in interpreting its performance.
  • PerformanceVector: Summarized the model's accuracy. The model achieved a general accuracy of 71.43%, meaning 71.43% of the predictions matched the actual outcomes.
  • SimpleDistribution (Naive Bayes): Displayed the probability of classes for the "Play" label. The class "no" had a probability of 0.357, while "yes" had a probability of 0.643. Four distributions were associated with each class, reflecting how the model categorized data.

Without Discretization:
To compare, I ran the model without using the "Numerical to Polynomial" operator. This allowed a direct comparison between the discretized and non-discretized processes.

The results were as follows:

Results:
Without discretization, the accuracy dropped from 71% to 64%, showing that discretization improved the performance of the Naive Bayes model.

Conclusion:
Comparing the confusion matrices from the discretized and non-discretized versions, we can observe key differences in overall accuracy:

  • With discretization: 71.43%
  • Without discretization: 64.29%
