UT4_TA10

Dataset: Sonar
In this task, we focused on the Sonar dataset. We started by visualizing the data using a Parallel Plot, which helped us identify areas where classes were more differentiated, notably between attributes 35 to 45.

We then switched the visualization to a Deviation Plot to further explore these differences.

Exercise 1

Modeling with Naive Bayes:
We used Naive Bayes for benchmarking and applied the same cross-validation techniques. Here are the summarized results:

  • PerformanceVector: Showed a general accuracy of 66.90%, indicating that 66.90% of the predictions matched the actual outcomes.
  • SimpleDistribution (Naive Bayes): Showed the probabilities for the "Rock" and "Mine" classes with probabilities of 0.466 and 0.534 respectively, across 60 distributions.
Exercise 2 and 3: Feature Selection

Using the "Multiply" operator, we applied "Forward Selection" in Exercise 2 and "Backward Elimination" in Exercise 3, each within a cross-validation block:

Results of Exercise 2:
Forward Selection improved the model's accuracy from 66.90% to 77.43%, highlighting the impact of selecting more influential attributes.

Results of Exercise 3:
Backward Elimination resulted in a slightly reduced accuracy of 73.12%, with detailed performance differences between the classes.

Comparison with previous exercises: In terms of overall accuracy, Backward Elimination resulted in slightly lower performance compared to Forward Selection (73.12% vs. 77.43%). The number of correctly predicted instances for the Rock class increased (82 compared to 67 in Forward Selection), but there was a decrease in correct predictions for the Mine class (70 compared to 94).

Results of Exercise 3:
Backward Elimination resulted in a slightly reduced accuracy of 73.12%, with detailed performance differences between the classes.

Exercise 4: Optimize Selection

Using the "Optimize Selection" algorithm, the model's accuracy improved significantly to 81.29%, selecting an optimal set of 9 attributes, which demonstrated an effective balance between simplicity and performance.

Conclusion:
The experiments illustrate the effectiveness of different feature selection techniques. Forward Selection streamlined the model by focusing on the most relevant features(4 attributes), while Backward Elimination retained more attributes(52) but did not significantly enhance performance. The "Optimize Selection" algorithm provided the best balance(9 attributes selected), resulting in the highest accuracy.
